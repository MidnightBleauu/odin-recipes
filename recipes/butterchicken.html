Butter Chicken

One of the most popular dishes in the world coming from indian cusine. This aromatic creamy curry sauce with tandoori chicken goes well with garlic buttered naan, and fresh basmatic rice

Ingredients

  • Greek or Non-fat Yoghurt
  • Boneless and skinless chicken thighs or breasts
  • 1/2 Plain Yoghurt
  • 1 tablespooon of minced ginger
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoons tumeric
  • 1 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic

    • Sauce Ingredients

  • 2 tablespooons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of butter or ghee
  • 1/2 tablespoon large onion, slice or chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced or finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 400grams of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 1/4 cup of heavy, thickended cream or coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek leaves

Cooking Instructions

  1. In a large bowl combine chicken with all the ingredients to create the marinade. (Leave covered for 30 minutes or overnight)
  2. Then get oil in a large skillet or pot over medium-high heat. Begin adding chicken in batches of two or three, and fry them in the pan until browned, but not fully cooked. For only 3 minutes at a time, once they are browned set aside to finish cooking with the sauce.
  3. Heat butter or ghee in the same pan, and fry the onions till they start to sweat. Which will take about six minutes. (I enjoy caramelizing my onions). Be sure to scrape any browned bits as well.
  4. Add your garlic and ginger and saute for about one minute, then add your ground coriander, cumin and garam masala. Let this cook for about 20 seconds until fragrant
  5. Take your crushed tomatoes, chilli poweder and salt and combine in the pot or pan to simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring until sauce thickens and becomes a deep brown colour.
  6. Remove the pan or pot from heat, and carefully put into a blender and blend until its smooth. Then run the blended sauce through a strainer, and it should be velvety smooth. Add water to assist in the blending process if necessary
  7. Then pour the sauce back into the pan while straining it through a fine mesh sieve, and stir in the cream, sugar and fenugreek leaves. Add the cooked chicken from earlier , and cook for 10-15 minutes. Until the chicken is cooked through, and the sauce is thick.
  8. Garish with cilantro, maybe some lime or lemon, and serve with some naan and basmatic rice.
  9. Eat and enjoy !
